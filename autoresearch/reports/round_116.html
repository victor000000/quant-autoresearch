Why

The drift ceiling (R90/R98/R100) was concluded with triple_barrier / crash-veto labels, which structurally under-return strong trends — they take profit at barriers mid-trend, giving back the carry. Trend-scanning instead labels each bar by the SIGN of its most-significant forward trend, so on a drifter it stays long through the uptrend (capturing carry) and only goes flat/short in real downtrends (cutting drawdown). That's exactly the asymmetry that beats buy-hold on a trender. The causal-graph analysis flagged the drift ETFs as under-explored (GLD/QQQ/HYG had 1-2 nodes each, ceilinged on shallow label trials); the right label reopens them.
