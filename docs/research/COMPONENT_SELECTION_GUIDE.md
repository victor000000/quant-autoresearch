# Component → ETF-type / Strategy-type selection guide

_Data-mined from results/round_results.csv (2,412 trials, 21 axes, 113 labeler strings, 125 tickers) joined to asset classes. Workflow: component-etf-strategy-map (wvbexpfn5), 2026-06-06._

## Summary

COMPONENT-SELECTION GUIDE synthesized from results/round_results.csv (2,412 trials, 21 axes, 113 labeler strings, 125 tickers) joined to the 125-row master verdict table and etf_qc asset classes. CORE FINDING: component choice is dictated by ASSET PHYSICS, not by tuning. Two regimes of asset classes exist — STRUCTURE-RICH (Commodity, Leveraged, Currency/FX, US-sector-equity) where active models add edge, vs BUY-HOLD-DOMINATED (broad Fixed-Income 14% fit, broad International-Equity 10% fit, REITs, broad-market SPY/QQQ) where always_long wins and any active mechanism only adds turnover and drawdown. Mechanism is selected by drift-beta: trend FITs sit at buy-hold-Calmar ~0.98 (moderate up-drift + trend-predictable deep drawdowns), regime FITs at ~0.34 (low-drift macro oscillators), reversion at ~0.66 (range-bound, no net drift), and buy-hold wins when β200>>0.6. Only TWO axes carry every confirmed edge: logdollar (universal info-clock, 27/37 crowns, ALL mechanisms, the ONLY reversion axis) and imbalance (the regime specialist, head-to-head dominates logdollar on UUP/GDX/SSO, the only non-logdollar deployed-book edge). The entire AFML microstructure-clock family (kyle/vpin/amihud/tickimb/volumeimb/tick) is DEAD on daily-rebalanced ETF minute bars (0 crowns, chance-level AUC). Labeler families by FIT-precision: TREND (trend_leg/ker, 51%, big clean edges, the workhorse), REVERSION (revert, 67%, oil-only, elite), META (triple_barrier_meta, 50%, equity-index-only), REGIME (bgm/sadf/changepoint, 38%, many crowns but mostly marginal edge ~0.21). Sizing: cdf_overlay is the universal default; dd_overlay is the winner ONLY on deep-drawdown metals/gold trend (GLD/GDX/XME/UGL); ls_overlay/longshort hurt up-drifters (median -0.001). CRITICAL caveat: round_results swept only AXIS x LABELER — reduce(corr/infogain/mrmr/autoencoder), features(base/rich/oscillators), and model(depth/ensemble/meta) are NOT columns; all conclusions on those three dimensions rest on ~5-6 controlled one-off A/Bs (GLD/IWM/TLT/UUP/DBC/EEM) in knowledge.json+memory and are MEDIUM/LOW confidence, several n=1. val_auc is necessary not sufficient: val_auc<0.52=reject; high-val_auc+low-edge=predictable-not-profitable trap (QQQ 0.969/HYG 0.981); high-Calmar+val_auc<0.56=drift/path-luck artifact; only beats-buy-hold + permute-collapse are valid arbiters.

## Decision procedure (route a NEW ETF)

- STEP 0 (cheap diagnostic, do FIRST): compute β200 = fraction of TRAIN bars with positive 200-bar forward return. β200>>0.6 (clean up-drift) => default to always_long BUY-HOLD; β200≈0.5 (no net drift / oscillating) => exploitable structure likely, proceed. β200 does NOT separate trend vs reversion vs regime — that requires running the labelers.
- STEP 1 (asset-class prior gate): if asset_class in {broad Fixed-Income, broad/diversified International-Equity, REIT, broad-market SPY/QQQ} AND β200>>0.6 => STOP, deploy always_long; active models here historically LOSE (FI trend positive in only 22% of trials; HYG regime auc .98 edge -1.30). Do not grind.
- STEP 2 (pick the axis from class): Commodity/metals/sector-equity/leveraged-LONG => START with logdollar. Currency/FX, oil-equity, S&P-leveraged-regime (SSO-type), gold-miners (GDX) => ALSO compute imbalance (it head-to-head dominates logdollar on regime names: UUP 2.54 vs 0.73, GDX 2.48 vs 1.78). Reversion candidates (oil futures) => logdollar ONLY. Skip the entire AFML microstructure family (kyle/vpin/amihud/tickimb/volumeimb/tick) — 0 crowns, chance-level AUC on daily ETF bars.
- STEP 3 (run BOTH mechanisms — β200 can't pick): race trend_leg/ker (trend), revert (reversion), and bgm+ker on imbalance (regime if imbalance shows structure). Choose the winner by edge_vs_bh, NOT by val_auc (revert structurally aces AUC even when unprofitable).
- STEP 4 (axis fallback): if logdollar trend val_auc stalls ~0.55 on a metals/gold-leveraged name, try vol (XME-type) and jump (gold-leveraged UGL) — these are the validated trend alternates that activate where logdollar misses structure.
- STEP 5 (mandatory deployment gate, all must pass): val_auc>0.52 AND real_calmar beats a NON-DEGENERATE buy-hold baseline (edge_vs_bh>0) AND permute-collapse confirms label signal AND trades>=30 (deployable). If buy-hold baseline is 0/undefined => NO-BASELINE artifact, REJECT regardless of Calmar.
- STEP 6 (artifact red-flags — auto-reject): best labeler peaks on IAU/QLD/BIL (no-baseline names); high Calmar at val_auc<0.56 without permute confirmation (drift/path-luck); high val_auc + edge<=0 (predictable-not-profitable, e.g. QQQ .969/HYG .981); sliced_wasserstein or standalone regime_gmm crown (artifact-prone, peak only on IAU/QLD).
- STEP 7 (reduce): correlation is the byte-identical default. Use infogain ONLY on a SINGLE trend-label component on a choppy/weak name with deflation headroom (GLD/IWM trend_leg). Do NOT use infogain on pure-regime ensembles (UUP IG 1.65<corr 1.85) or clean drifters (DBC IG 0.45<corr 0.59). Never use autoencoder (REFUTED 2.44->1.11). mrmr is untested (0 deployed runs).
- STEP 8 (features): base 80-panel (+entropy) is the proven default. Add ③ RSI+Bollinger %b oscillators ONLY for a mean-reverting asset paired with a triple_barrier labeler on range/imbalance axis (TLT 0.31->1.52, IWM 0.88->0.93); they HURT on logdollar-trend and regime. Never add rich/VR/FFD/cross-asset features under correlation (crowd-out: FFD destroyed TLT 1.52->-0.001).
- STEP 9 (model): XGBoost depth 3 (deeper overfits — capacity-adds consistently hurt: AE, sample-uniqueness). Ensemble ONLY two comparable, decorrelated, SOFT-label (bgm) legs (IWM 0.93->1.14); 3+ legs or strong+weak pairs DILUTE; hard regime legs (kmeans/regime_gmm/cusum) go 0-trade degenerate as a blend leg. Meta-labeling (secondary gate) ONLY on a two-sided index with a STRONG long-biased primary, and it is LONG-ONLY (HURT TLT L/S 1.52->0.59).
- STEP 10 (sizing): cdf_overlay is the universal default. Switch to dd_overlay (drawdown overlay) ONLY on deep-drawdown metals/gold trend names (empirically GLD/GDX/XME/UGL all win on dd_overlay). Never deploy ls_overlay/longshort sizing on an up-drifting asset — median real_calmar -0.001 (shorts give back the drift).
- STEP 11 (re-validate before trusting): stored Calmars/book go stale as the OOS window grows; weak timing edges (EEM/TLT) decay while drift edges (GLD) hold. Re-run the deterministic pipeline before deploying any historical crown.

## Dead combinations (proven NOT to work)

- revert x QQQ (and any up-drifter): val_auc 0.97 but edge -0.03 — reversion is equally predictable but buy-hold captures the drift with zero turnover (NO-FIT trap).
- trend (trend_leg/ker) x broad Fixed-Income: positive in only 22% of trials; BSV trend edge -2.93, IYZ -1.13, JNK -1.17 — active trend on clean bond drifters loses badly.
- regime (cusum_regime/changepoint/sadf) x HYG: val_auc 0.98 (perfectly predictable) but edge -1.30 — the canonical predictable-not-profitable trap.
- AFML microstructure clocks (kyle, vpin, amihud, tickimb, volumeimb, tick) x ENTIRE universe: 0 per-ETF crowns; best-trial val_auc at/below chance (tick .46, tickimb .36, volumeimb .50, vpin .53, kyle .59). Dead on daily-rebalanced ETF minute bars.
- dollar (plain, un-logged) x everything: 0 crowns, strictly dominated by logdollar — the log transform is load-bearing.
- sliced_wasserstein x IAU & QLD: both master crowns are verdict=artifact (no-baseline names); peaks exclusively on unverifiable tickers, median Calmar 0.000.
- regime_gmm STANDALONE x IAU: high AUC (med .902) but median Calmar 0.000 and deployable ceiling lands on no-baseline IAU 3.34 — use only as a gate add-on to a trend labeler, never alone.
- trend_leg+regime_gmm x low-vol/cash-like names: manufactures BIL 30.43 (T-bill cash artifact), VWO 1.53 & IGE (buyhold=0) — only 1/6 of its crowns is a real FIT.
- sticky_hmm x GLD: val_auc 0.8785 (highly predictable) yet Calmar only 0.40 vs 4.71 from ker+regime_gmm on the same asset — predictable-but-not-profitable, dominated everywhere.
- autoencoder reduce x any name: nonlinear AE 1.11 << linear corr-select 2.44 (overfits/adds noise — 'we SELECT, not PROJECT').
- infogain reduce x UUP regime ensemble (1.65 < corr 1.85) and x DBC clean drifter (0.45 < corr 0.59 < BH 0.91) — IG helps single trend-label components, not regime ensembles or drifters.
- rich/FFD/cross-asset features x TLT under correlation: FFD destroyed the edge 1.52->-0.001; SPY cross-asset regressed 0.768->0.677 — feature crowd-out (TLT needs PAIRS not more features).
- ③ oscillator features x logdollar-trend (GLD/TLT 0.77->0.41) and x regime(bgm): oscillators are mean-reverter+barrier-specific, not a global add.
- meta-labeling x TLT long/short (1.52->0.59, gate flattens directional calls & kills short profit) and x IWM weak-primary (1.14->0.20) — needs a STRONG long-biased primary.
- AFML sample-uniqueness weighting x EEM (2.43->1.77) and x TLT (1.52->-0.14): down-weighting overlapping samples HURTS directional edges — overlap IS the persistence signal.
- 3+ leg ensembles x TLT (0.77->0.23), EEM (2.15->1.39), GLD (trend_scan+2nd 2.16->1.80), IWM 3-way (0.87) — dilution when one labeler already dominates.
- hard regime labelers (kmeans2stage/regime_gmm/cusum) as a STANDALONE ensemble blend leg: go degenerate (0 trades, val_auc .39) and drag consensus below threshold.
- ls_overlay / longshort sizing x up-drifting assets: median real_calmar -0.001 / 0.0 — shorts give back the drift on names that trend up.
- trend x inverse/short ETFs (PSQ/SPXS/QID/SH/SDS) for DEPLOYMENT: positive edge_vs_bh only because buy-hold is deeply negative; ABSOLUTE Calmar stays ~0 — real but not deployable.
- sharpe_scan/sortino_scan/turn_scan/agglomerative/carry labelers x anything: never crown a clean FIT (sharpe_scan medCal -0.02, agglomerative 0.01, carry sub-20-trade TLT-only).

## Data gaps & caveats

"BIGGEST GAP — three of the five pipeline dimensions are essentially unswept in the large grid. results/round_results.csv carries only AXIS (21) x LABELER columns; it has NO reduce / features / max_depth / ensemble-arity columns (grep-confirmed: zero infogain/rich/mrmr/depth tokens in axis/labeler fields). ALL conclusions about REDUCE (corr/infogain/autoencoder/mrmr), FEATURES (base/rich/③oscillators/VR/FFD), and MODEL (depth/ensemble/meta) rest on ~5-6 controlled one-off A/B pairs in knowledge.json+memory (GLD, IWM, TLT, UUP, DBC, EEM) — MEDIUM/LOW confidence, several are single-name (n=1). These need a proper grid sweep before they can be trusted at the level of the axis/labeler slices. (a) mrmr reduce: built in trainer.py but ZERO deployed runs — completely untested. (b) max_depth: depth-5-overfits is a stated PRIOR with no in-data A/B; only indirect support (AE + sample-uniqueness capacity-adds hurt). (c) reduce=infogain validated on only ~5 A/Bs. TRIAL-BUDGET SKEW: logdollar got 1654/2412 trials (69%), imbalance 263 (11%), the other 19 axes only ~20-52 each (a single deep-sweep pass) — so 'dead axis' means 'no edge in a thin sweep' EXCEPT on the commodity/currency/leveraged fit-classes where all 21 axes explicitly ran (there the dead axes are well-tested-dead). THIN ASSET CLASSES: Currency FIT rests on N=4 tickers (UUP/FXF FIT, FXE artifact, FXY thin) — directionally strong but low confidence on rate; REVERSION rests on 2 oil tickers / 37 trials; META on 2 equity indices (EEM/IWM); Volatility on 1 ticker (VIXY artifact) — NO conclusion possible. SIZING under-explored: cdf_overlay (1426) and dd_overlay (794) are well-sampled, but crashveto (n=16), binary (n=12), cdf_plain (n=10), longshort (n=6), ddbreaker (n=1) are too thin to rank — crashveto's high median (1.66 at trades>=30) is suggestive but unproven. LEADERBOARD STALENESS: stored Calmars/book go stale as the OOS window grows (weak EEM/TLT timing edges decay, GLD drift holds) — re-validate before trusting any historical crown. NO EXTERNAL DATA: no VIX/cross-asset feeds ingested; the SPY crash-veto signal (jump axis + crash_ahead) is promising but not deployable (73<80 trades, threshold-fragile) and stalls without VIX data. NO CROSS-SECTIONAL/PAIRS path: the entire study is single-ticker; the no-new-data escape (cross-sectional spread, e.g. TLT needs PAIRS) is identified in Wang investigation but unbuilt."
