## AXISS
### amihud [NEW]
- idea: Amihud-illiquidity (price-impact-per-DOLLAR) clock. Accumulate |log-return| / (close*volume) = absolute return per dollar of notional traded, and emit a bar when the cumulative Amihud impact reaches a target. Bars close fast when price moves a lot on little DOLLAR volume (illiquid / informed) and slow when big notional barely moves price (deep, liquid). This is the canonical Amihud (2002) ILLIQ measure used as a sampling clock. IMPORTANT distinction from the existing `kyle` axis: kyle accumulates |dlogprice|/sqrt(volume) (a Kyle-lambda / square-root-of-SHARE-volume impact). Amihud divides by DOLLAR volume close*vol (linear, not sqrt; notional, not shares). Empirically Amihud and Kyle-lambda rank illiquidity differently and are only weakly correlated, so this is a genuinely different illiquidity clock -- it removes the price-level and uses the linear-dollar denominator that the asset-pricing literature actually prices. For thinly-but-expensively-traded regimes (rate shocks in TLT, flight days in GLD) the dollar denominator behaves differently from the sqrt-share denominator.
- mechanism: Streaming, O(1), mirrors the existing KyleImpactBarBuilder but with a DOLLAR denominator. On each minute: contrib = |lc_t - lc_{t-1}| / (close_t * vol_t) (guard close>0, vol>0). cum += contrib; emit a bar (reset) when cum >= threshold. Threshold auto-calibrated exactly like kyle/logdollar: TRAIN mean of contrib per valid minute, extrapolated by TRAIN-valid-density to full length / target_bars (the OOS-invariant rate-on-TRAIN recipe already in _make_builder for kyle, avoiding the full-series-sum leak). Purely past/current prices and volumes -> causal. Only the threshold is fit (TRAIN). To keep it numerically tame the contrib can be scaled by a constant (e.g. 1e9) since dollar volume is large; scaling is monotone and cancels in calibration.
- source: Amihud, Y. (2002), 'Illiquidity and stock returns: cross-section and time-series effects', Journal of Financial Markets 5(1):31-56. PDF: https://www.cis.upenn.edu/~mkearns/finread/amihud.pdf . Intraday/realized-Amihud sampling: realized-illiquidity literature (AFA). Distinct from Kyle (1985) lambda which the repo's `kyle` axis approximates.

### dyn_threshold (rolling/recomputed-monthly bar threshold) [NEW]
- idea: Not a new accumulation VARIABLE but a new THRESHOLD POLICY usable on top of any existing size axis (dollar/vol/tick): recompute the emission threshold periodically (e.g. monthly) from trailing turnover/$-volume so the bar FREQUENCY stays constant as the asset's turnover drifts over a 17-year backtest. Distinct from inventory: every current axis uses a single STATIC threshold = total/target_bars fit once; over 17 years turnover drifts by orders of magnitude, silently under/over-sampling the late period. AFML Ch.2 explicitly warns the static-dollar-bar threshold breaks and recommends a rolling threshold. This is borderline (a policy, not a fresh axis) but materially changes sampling and is genuinely not implemented.
- mechanism: threshold_m = trailing_12mo_avg_daily_dollar_volume / target_bars_per_day, recomputed at each month boundary using ONLY data up to that month (strictly causal, expanding/rolling -> leak-safe). Apply the current threshold to the streaming accumulator until the next recompute. ~20 lines inside the existing DollarBarBuilder/VolBarBuilder.
- source: Lopez de Prado AFML Ch.2 (§2.3.1.4, 'sample dollar bars where the size of the bar is not kept constant over time'); technique_catalog.md §①/① 'Dynamic (rolling) dollar-bar threshold' (Pri 4)

### ewma_imbalance_bar (TIB/VIB/DIB with EWMA-expectation threshold) [NEW]
- idea: AFML Ch.2.3.2.1-2.3.2.2 in its FULL form. Our imbalance/tickimb/volumeimb axes implement the signed-flow accumulator θ but compare it to a FIXED (TRAIN-fit constant) threshold. De Prado's actual definition compares |θ_T| to its dynamically-updated EXPECTATION E0[θ_T]=E0[T]·|2P[b=1]−1| (TIB) or E0[T]·|2v^+−E0[v]| (VIB/DIB), estimated as EWMAs of T and of b_t·v_t over prior bars. So the bar emits 'more frequently under the presence of informed trading' relative to the asset's OWN recent imbalance baseline — a self-normalizing information clock. The adaptive EWMA threshold is the missing ingredient vs our fixed-threshold versions; it removes the need to refit when the imbalance regime drifts.
- mechanism: θ_T = Σ b_t (TIB) or Σ b_t·v_t (VIB/DIB), b_t tick-rule sign. Expectation E0[θ_T] = E0[T]·|2P[b=1]−1| (TIB) or E0[T]·|2v^+ − E0[v_t]| (VIB/DIB), with E0[T]=EWMA of prior bar lengths and the (2P−1)/(2v^+−E0[v]) term = EWMA of b_t (or b_t·v_t) over prior bars. Emit when |θ_T| ≥ E0[θ_T]. Seed EWMAs on TRAIN, update online (causal). Differs from inventory ONLY in the adaptive threshold — implement as a variant flag on the existing imbalance builders. ~35 lines.
- source: AFML Ch.2.3.2.1 Tick Imbalance Bars + 2.3.2.2 Volume/Dollar Imbalance Bars (E0[θ_T] EWMA rule). /tmp/afml_full.txt lines 140-211

### fluss_arc [NEW]
- idea: Matrix-profile semantic-segmentation bar AXIS. Maintain a streaming matrix-profile arc-curve (FLUSS): emit a new bar each time the arc-count (number of nearest-neighbor 'arcs' crossing the current index) dips below a TRAIN-calibrated threshold — i.e. sample a bar at every detected SEMANTIC regime boundary, not on the clock. This concentrates bars at structural change-points, the opposite emphasis to the existing dc/zcusum axes (which fire on price-move magnitude) — FLUSS fires on SHAPE/pattern dissimilarity. New vs all 15 axes: none uses subsequence self-similarity. Online & domain-agnostic by construction (FLUSS is the streaming variant of FLOSS), TRAIN-fit only the arc threshold and subsequence length.
- mechanism: Streaming: keep a sliding window of recent length-m subsequences; for the current index maintain the count of arcs (pointers from each subsequence to its nearest neighbor) that cross it; corrected-arc-curve CAC dips at regime boundaries. Emit a bar when CAC < cut. cut and m fit on TRAIN arc-curve distribution; applied forward unchanged. Implement with an online z-normalized distance profile (STOMP/streaming) ~60 lines, or approximate with a windowed 1-NN arc count for the ~50-line budget. Citation: Gharghabi et al. (2017) 'Matrix Profile VIII: Domain Agnostic Online Semantic Segmentation' (FLUSS/FLOSS), ICDM.
- source: https://stumpy.readthedocs.io/en/latest/Tutorial_Semantic_Segmentation.html

### jump [NEW]
- idea: Lee-Mykland intraday jump-detection clock. Standardize each new bar/minute return by a LOCAL bipower-variation volatility (which is jump-robust), and emit a bar whenever the standardized return crosses a jump-significance threshold OR whenever accumulated standardized energy reaches a target. This samples the price path on the JUMP clock: it fires fast exactly at discontinuities (news/FOMC/earnings shocks) and slow during smooth diffusion. Orthogonal to every existing axis: vol/dollar/logdollar speed up on SIZE (ret^2*sqrt(vol), close*vol), imbalance/run on DIRECTIONAL RUNS, dc/spectral on REVERSALS/CYCLES, zcusum on standardized log-DOLLAR cumsum. None standardizes the move against a JUMP-ROBUST (bipower, not quadratic) local vol, which is the whole point of Lee-Mykland: quadratic vol is contaminated by the jump itself, bipower is not, so the |L| statistic is a clean jump signal. For a two-sided or event-driven asset (TLT rate shocks, semis gap days) this concentrates bars on the informative discontinuities the labelers (ker/trend_scan) need, and de-clocks the smooth drift.
- mechanism: Streaming, O(1) with a ring buffer of the last K-2 absolute returns. On each new log-return r_i = lc_i - lc_{i-1}: maintain rolling bipower instantaneous variance over the trailing window, sigma_hat^2(i) = (1/(K-2)) * sum_{j=i-K+2}^{i-1} |r_j| * |r_{j-1}| (Lee-Mykland eq.8; product of CONSECUTIVE absolute returns -> jump-robust). Statistic L(i) = r_i / sigma_hat(i) (eq.7). EMIT a bar when |L(i)| >= T, then (optionally) continue; T is set so ~target_bars fire. Two leak-safe calibration options: (a) fixed Gumbel critical value: under the diffusive null, max_i |L(i)| follows a Gumbel with location C_n and scale S_n where c=sqrt(2/pi)=0.7979, C_n=(2 log n)^{1/2}/c - (log pi + log log n)/(2 c (2 log n)^{1/2}), S_n=1/(c (2 log n)^{1/2}); reject (=emit) when |L(i)| > C_n + S_n*beta_star with beta_star=-log(-log(1-alpha)); (b) simpler and matches the repo's auto-calibration style: binary-search a single threshold T on TRAIN |L| values so the TRAIN emission count = TRAIN share of target_bars (exactly like _fit_run_axis / _fit_spectral_axis). K frozen (paper recommends K=270 for 5-min, ~78 for 1-hour; pick K by target bar coarseness). Everything is trailing-window -> strictly causal; only K and T are parameters, both TRAIN-fit. Bipower in the denominator means an earlier jump does not corrupt the current vol estimate (the repo's existing vol axis uses quadratic ret^2 and would be corrupted).
- source: Lee, S.S. & Mykland, P.A. (2008), 'Jumps in Financial Markets: A New Nonparametric Test and Jump Dynamics', Review of Financial Studies 21(6):2535-2563. Read directly: https://galton.uchicago.edu/~mykland/paperlinks/LeeMykland-2535.pdf (eqs 6,7,8; Theorem 1 constant c=sqrt(2/pi); K recommendations p.2542). Bipower variation foundation: Barndorff-Nielsen & Shephard (2004/2006).

### normality_axis (Jarque-Bera / kurtosis-optimized emission clock) [NEW]
- idea: A meta-axis: instead of a single accumulation rule, TUNE the emission rule's threshold on TRAIN to MINIMIZE the Jarque-Bera statistic / excess-kurtosis of the resulting bar-return distribution (drive bar diffs toward Gaussian). Wang's Module 3 explicitly lists '实现一阶差分接近正态分布的其它自定义轴' (other custom axes whose first-difference approaches a normal distribution) as a deliverable, and the Wang Q&A Q1 is entirely about peak_frac/Gaussianity of bar returns. Distinct from inventory: every existing axis fixes its accumulation variable a priori (dollar, range, entropy, etc.) and the threshold is just total/target_bars; NONE selects/tunes the threshold (or blends accumulation variables) by a normality objective. Near-Gaussian IID bars make XGBoost splits, calibration and sizing valid OOS -> the unifying axis-selection criterion the catalog says is absent.
- mechanism: Candidate accumulation rule f (e.g. a convex blend a*dollar + (1-a)*range, or a power-law dollar^p). Sweep the free parameter on TRAIN minutes; for each, build bars, compute scipy.stats.jarque_bera (or excess kurtosis / peak_frac) of bar log-returns, subject to bar-count >= target. Pick the parameter minimizing JB on TRAIN; freeze it and apply forward. Objective + threshold are TRAIN-only -> leak-safe. ~30 lines wrapping an existing builder + scipy.stats.jarque_bera/kurtosis.
- source: Wang course slides Module 3 ('实现一阶差分接近正态分布的其它自定义轴'); Wang transcript uVnOeOcoivw l.460-775; Wang QA Q1 (peak_frac); technique_catalog.md §①/② 'Normality-optimized custom axis' & 'Per-asset axis selection by kurtosis/peak' (Pri 5/4)

### renko_atr (ATR-adaptive Renko / constant-price-move brick clock) [NEW]
- idea: Emit a bar every time price moves one BRICK = k * ATR from the last brick, where the brick size is recomputed from trailing ATR (so it adapts to the asset's volatility regime). This filters flat chop (no bar emitted in quiet periods) and homogenizes the return SCALE per bar. Distinct from inventory: the existing 'range' axis emits on a FIXED percentage move; 'vol' axis accumulates ret^2*sqrt(vol) (a variance integral, not a discrete price-brick); 'dc' (directional-change) keys off reversal thresholds, not constant unidirectional bricks. ATR-adaptive Renko is a constant-information PRICE clock whose brick floats with volatility - a refinement none of the 15 axes implement.
- mechanism: Maintain last_brick_price. brick = k * ATR_trailing (ATR computed on a trailing window of completed minutes/bars; k a small int). On each minute: while |close - last_brick_price| >= brick, emit a bar at last_brick_price +/- brick and step last_brick_price by one brick toward close (can emit multiple bricks on a gap). ATR window length + k fit on TRAIN (or k set, ATR auto from TRAIN trailing) -> threshold is TRAIN-derived, no full-series/OOS stats -> leak-safe. Native LEAN ClassicRenkoConsolidator is a zero-custom-code alternative. ~25 lines.
- source: Lopez de Prado AFML Ch.2; QC docs (ClassicRenko/VolumeRenko consolidators); Gradzki FI 2025; technique_catalog.md §① row 'Range/Renko (constant price-move), brick = k·ATR' (Pri 4)

### roughness [NEW]
- idea: Roughness / Hurst-change (fractal-dimension) clock. Maintain a rolling rescaled-range (R/S) estimate of the LOCAL Hurst exponent H of the recent log-price path and emit a bar each time H crosses between regimes -- i.e. each time the path's character flips between trending (H>0.5, persistent), random-walk (H~0.5), and mean-reverting/rough (H<0.5, anti-persistent). This samples on REGIME ROUGHNESS rather than on size, flow, or cycle. It is distinct from everything in the registry: fracdiff samples on memory-bearing stationarized PRICE increments (a fixed d, fixed filter), but does NOT measure or react to the path's local fractal dimension; spectral fires on a fixed frequency band; this fires when the persistence/anti-persistence structure itself changes. Modern rough-volatility evidence (H<<0.5 for vol) and the practical 'Hurst regime' literature motivate a clock that densely samples regime transitions -- exactly the points where a trend-momentum labeler's edge appears or vanishes -- and sparsely samples within a stable regime.
- mechanism: Streaming with a trailing window of W log-returns (ring buffer). Periodically (every step or every m steps) compute the rolling Hurst via rescaled-range over the window: for the windowed return series x, mean-adjust y = x - mean(x), cumulative deviate Z_k = sum_{j<=k} y_j, range R = max(Z) - min(Z), S = std(x); rescaled range R/S. Single-window H estimate H_hat = log(R/S) / log(W) (the log-log R/S slope; for a streaming O(W) estimate use the single-scale ratio, or a small dyadic set of sub-window scales for a regression slope). Track H_hat with a small hysteresis band around 0.5 (e.g. enter-trend at 0.55, enter-revert at 0.45). EMIT a bar on each regime CROSSING (trend<->randomwalk<->revert), then continue. Calibration: the only knobs are W and the band; choose W and band on TRAIN by simulation so the TRAIN crossing count = TRAIN share of target_bars (binary-search the band half-width, identical pattern to _fit_spectral_axis / _fit_run_axis). Strictly trailing window -> causal; fit on TRAIN only. (Cost O(W) per emit-check; with m-step subsampling it is cheap.)
- source: Hurst, H.E. (1951) R/S analysis; Mandelbrot & Wallis (1969) fractal dimension. Rough-volatility: Gatheral, Jaisson & Rosenbaum (2018) 'Volatility is rough', Quantitative Finance 18(6):933-949. Rolling-Hurst regime detection (practitioner): FractalCycles 'Rolling Hurst Exponent' guide; Macrosynergy 'Detecting trends and mean reversion with the Hurst exponent'. Adjusted-R/S small-sample correction: Lo (1991); arXiv:1805.08931.

### runs_bar (tick/volume/dollar RUNS bars, EWMA-expectation) [NEW]
- idea: AFML Ch.2.3.2.3-2.3.2.4 (TRB/VRB/DRB). NOT the same as our `run` axis (which accumulates within-run |log-return| MAGNITUDE and resets on every sign flip) nor our imbalance axes (which net signed flow, offsetting buys against sells). AFML runs bars count the same-side ticks/volume WITHOUT offsetting (sequence breaks allowed), and crucially emit when that count exceeds its EWMA-of-prior-bars EXPECTATION max{P[b=1]·E[v|buy], (1−P[b=1])·E[v|sell]} — an adaptive, self-calibrating threshold, not a fixed one. This 'buckets of equal information' clock fires when one side's run-volume is anomalously persistent vs its own recent history. The EWMA-expectation emission rule is the genuinely new mechanism (none of our axes adapt the threshold to recent bar history).
- mechanism: Tick rule sign b_t. Run accumulator θ_T = max(Σ_{b=+1} 1, Σ_{b=-1} 1) for TRB, or θ_T = max(Σ_{b=+1} v_t, Σ_{b=-1} v_t) for VRB/DRB (count each side separately, no offset). Expected θ at bar start: E0[T]·max{P[b=1]·E0[v|+], (1−P[b=1])·E0[v|−]}, where E0[T], P[b=1], E0[v|±] are EWMAs over PRIOR bars (causal, no future). Emit when θ_T ≥ expectation. Seed E0[T], P, E0[v|±] from TRAIN bars; update EWMA online → leak-safe streaming builder. ~50 lines.
- source: AFML Ch.2.3.2.3 Tick Runs Bars + 2.3.2.4 Volume/Dollar Runs Bars. /tmp/afml_full.txt lines 213-273

### toxicity [NEW]
- idea: VPIN / order-flow-toxicity clock (Easley-Lopez de Prado-O'Hara). Run de Prado's Bulk Volume Classification (BVC) to split each minute's volume into estimated BUY vs SELL volume using the standardized price change through a normal CDF, accumulate the signed order imbalance, and emit a bar when cumulative |imbalance| reaches a target -> i.e. bars close fast when flow is TOXIC (one-sided, adversely-selected) and slow when flow is balanced. This is genuinely distinct from the existing imbalance/volumeimb/tickimb axes: THOSE use the hard tick-rule sign (+1/-1 carry-forward) times notional/shares/1. BVC instead assigns a SOFT, probabilistic buy fraction Z(dP/sigma_dP) in (0,1) per bar -- a single sharp move is scored as ~100% informed buys, a flat move as 50/50 -- which is the actual VPIN toxicity construction and reacts to the MAGNITUDE-scaled directionality, not just the sign. It is the literature's canonical 'informed-trading' event clock and the natural microstructure complement to the size/run clocks already built.
- mechanism: Streaming, O(1). Maintain a trailing estimate of sigma_dP = std of per-minute log-price changes (causal rolling, e.g. EWMA or ring-buffer; can also be frozen from TRAIN). On each minute with log-return dP = lc_t - lc_{t-1}: buy fraction f = Z(dP / sigma_dP) where Z = standard normal CDF (Student-t in the original; normal is the common simplification). V_buy = vol * f, V_sell = vol * (1 - f). Per-minute signed imbalance contribution = |V_buy - V_sell| = vol * |2f - 1|. Accumulate cum += vol*|2f-1|; emit a bar (reset cum) when cum >= threshold. Threshold auto-calibrated like dollar/logdollar: TRAIN mean of vol*|2f-1| per minute * full length / target_bars (rate-on-TRAIN-then-scale, OOS-invariant). (True VPIN = average of bucket imbalances / V over n buckets; here we use the imbalance accumulator directly AS the sampling clock, which is the bar-builder analogue.) sigma_dP and the threshold are TRAIN-fit; Z is parameter-free -> causal and leak-safe. Note Z is the smooth-CDF soft sign, the key difference vs the hard-sign imbalance axes already in the registry.
- source: Easley, D., Lopez de Prado, M., O'Hara, M. (2012), 'Flow Toxicity and Liquidity in a High-Frequency World', Review of Financial Studies 25(5):1457-1493 (VPIN + Bulk Volume Classification). SSRN: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=1695596 ; quantresearch.org/VPIN.pdf. BVC detail: Easley, Lopez de Prado, O'Hara (2016) 'Discerning Information from Trade Data', J. Financial Economics.

### volofvol [NEW]
- idea: Realized-volatility-of-volatility clock. Estimate a rolling SPOT volatility (jump-robust, via threshold/bipower realized variance over a short trailing window), then accumulate the realized variation OF that spot-vol series, and emit a bar when the cumulative vol-of-vol reaches a target. Bars close fast when volatility itself is moving rapidly (vol regime turning, vol-spikes, vol-crush) and slow when vol is steady -- even if the level of vol is high. This is orthogonal to the existing `vol` axis: vol bars sample equal amounts of realized VARIANCE (level of vol); volofvol bars sample equal amounts of CHANGE-in-vol (the second-order, vol-of-vol clock). The vol-of-vol / rough-vol literature shows vol-of-vol carries distinct predictive content (it spikes around regime shifts and stress). A clock tuned to vol-acceleration concentrates bars at volatility regime boundaries -- the timing edge that EEM/TLT-style assets carry and that level-vol bars smear across.
- mechanism: Streaming, two-stage, O(1) with two ring buffers. Stage 1 (spot vol): over a trailing window of length K_v, maintain a jump-robust spot-variance estimate sigma2_t = (pi/2) * (1/(K_v-1)) * sum |r_j||r_{j-1}| (bipower; optionally THRESHOLD-truncated a la Corsi-Pirino-Reno: drop r_j^2 terms exceeding c*sigma2 to remove jumps). Stage 2 (vol-of-vol): let v_t = log(sigma2_t) (log so it is scale-free and additive); accumulate cum += |v_t - v_{t-1}| (total variation of log-spot-vol = realized vol-of-vol proxy). EMIT a bar when cum >= threshold, reset. Calibration: threshold = TRAIN mean of |v_t - v_{t-1}| per minute * full length / target_bars (rate-on-TRAIN-then-scale, OOS-invariant), K_v and the bipower constant pi/2 frozen. All trailing-window -> causal; only K_v and threshold are TRAIN-fit. Uses bipower (not quadratic) in stage 1 so a single jump does not masquerade as a vol-of-vol event (the threshold version is even cleaner).
- source: Barndorff-Nielsen, O.E. & Veraart, A. (2009/2013) 'Stochastic volatility of volatility and variance risk premia' (integrated vol-of-vol = realized vol of estimated spot vols). Corsi, F., Pirino, D., Reno, R. (2010) 'Threshold Bipower Variation and the Impact of Jumps on Volatility Forecasting', J. Econometrics (threshold bipower, jump-robust spot vol): https://hal.science/hal-00741630v1/document . Vol-of-vol estimation under noise+jumps: Sanfelici et al., J. Econometrics 2021. Bipower foundation: Barndorff-Nielsen & Shephard (2004).

### vpin_bvc (VPIN / bulk-volume-classified imbalance bars) [NEW]
- idea: AFML Ch.19.5.2 + Ch.2.3.2: the flagged-as-missed method. Our existing imbalance/tickimb/volumeimb axes split each minute into buy/sell with the HARD tick rule (±1 on sign of price change). VPIN's bulk-volume classification (BVC) instead splits each bar's volume PROBABILISTICALLY: buy-fraction = Z(Δlogprice/σ) via the standard-normal CDF, so a +0.3σ move is ~62% buy / 38% sell rather than 100% buy. The bar emits when the rolling order-flow toxicity VPIN = Σ|V_buy−V_sell|/ΣV crosses a threshold — sampling densely under one-sided/toxic flow (informed trading) and sparsely in balanced two-way flow. Genuinely new: probabilistic (not hard-sign) classification AND a toxicity-accumulation clock, neither of which exists in the inventory. Well-suited to minute ETF bars where there is no true aggressor side, since BVC was designed exactly for bar data without tick-level signs.
- mechanism: Per minute bar: standardized return z=Δlog(close)/σ_train; buy-volume V^B = vol·Φ(z), sell-volume V^S = vol·(1−Φ(z)) where Φ is the standard-normal CDF (BVC; Easley-Lopez de Prado-O'Hara 2012). Maintain rolling sums over the last n volume buckets: VPIN = Σ|V^B−V^S| / Σ(V^B+V^S). Emit a bar when cumulative |V^B−V^S| ≥ threshold (DIB/VIB analogue with soft signs), OR sample on VPIN crossing. σ_train fit on TRAIN minutes only; bar threshold from TRAIN minute count (no full-series/OOS stats) → leak-safe streaming builder, O(1)/update with a ring buffer. ~45 lines.
- source: AFML Ch.19.5.2 VPIN (Easley-Lopez de Prado-O'Hara 2011/2012) + Ch.2.3.2 imbalance bars + bulk-volume classification. /tmp/afml_full.txt lines ~8940-8972 and 2.3.2

### wavelet [NEW]
- idea: Wavelet / multiresolution detail-energy clock. Run a causal, undecimated (a-trous / stationary) wavelet filter bank on the streaming log-price, isolating one or two DETAIL scales (a chosen frequency band), accumulate the squared detail coefficients (the band-localized ENERGY), and emit a bar when cumulative energy in that band reaches a target. Bars close fast when the price path injects energy at the targeted scale (e.g. fast oscillations / micro-shocks at fine scales, or swings at coarse scales) and slow otherwise. This generalizes and is distinct from the existing `spectral` axis: spectral is a SINGLE 2-EMA band-pass and fires on its ZERO-CROSSINGS (timing of half-cycles); the wavelet axis instead accumulates ENERGY across one or more dyadic scales and fires on energy budget -- it samples by how much MULTIRESOLUTION variance has accrued in a band, with proper orthogonal scale separation (MODWT partitions variance exactly by scale). It is the multi-scale energy analogue of the vol clock, letting the downstream model see bars equalized in band-specific information rather than total notional or total variance.
- mechanism: Streaming via the a-trous (stationary wavelet transform) algorithm, which is causal and shift-invariant -- the right choice for online bars (no decimation, no future samples). Use a short Haar or Daubechies low-pass h; at each new sample compute smooth approximations at dyadic scales by inserting 2^(j-1)-1 zeros between filter taps (a-trous): A_0 = lc_t; A_j[t] = sum_k h_k * A_{j-1}[t - 2^(j-1) k] (taps reach only into the PAST -> causal). Detail at scale j: D_j[t] = A_{j-1}[t] - A_j[t]. Pick target scale(s) J (a frequency band). Accumulate cum += D_J[t]^2 (band energy; sum over a chosen set of scales for a band). EMIT a bar when cum >= threshold, reset. Calibration: threshold = TRAIN mean band-energy per minute * full length / target_bars (OOS-invariant rate-on-TRAIN); the filter h, the scale set J, and the buffer depth (2^J taps of history) are frozen. Only past samples enter each A_j -> strictly causal; only the threshold is TRAIN-fit. (MODWT/a-trous gives an exact scale-by-scale variance partition, so the energy accumulator is a clean band-variance clock.)
- source: Percival, D.B. & Walden, A.T. (2000) 'Wavelet Methods for Time Series Analysis' (MODWT, scale-by-scale variance partition). A-trous / stationary wavelet transform (shift-invariant, causal): Shensa (1992); Nason & Silverman (1995). Financial high/low-energy wavelet decomposition: 'Option Pricing from Wavelet-Filtered Financial Series', arXiv:1103.3639; Gencay, Selcuk & Whitcher (2002) 'An Introduction to Wavelets and Other Filtering Methods in Finance'. MODWT MRA reference: MathWorks modwtmra.

## LABELS
### calmar_scan [NEW]
- idea: Drawdown-adjusted forward trend = net forward move divided by the WORST peak-to-trough drawdown INSIDE the forward window (path MaxDD), not by vol. Directly the Calmar objective the book already optimizes (CAGR/MaxDD), pushed down to the label: a long is 'good' only if it reaches its destination without a deep intra-path drawdown. Distinct from mfe_mae (which uses MAE = drop from the START, not the running peak) and from sharpe_scan/sortino_scan (vol denominators): Calmar's denominator is the maximum running drawdown, the trough relative to the highest point reached so far inside the window. This is the path-risk measure most aligned with the deployment metric.
- mechanism: Per bar t, horizon H: path = lc[t..t+H]; running_peak = np.maximum.accumulate(path); dd = running_peak - path; MaxDD = max(dd); net = path[-1]-path[0]; CMR = net / (MaxDD + eps). Label 1 if |CMR|>=cut and net>0, 0 if net<=0, -1 else. cut = TRAIN quantile of |CMR|. Per-bar inner loop over H (or rolling-peak trick); ~45 lines. Citation: Young (1991), 'Calmar Ratio: A Smoother Tool', Futures; CAGR/|MaxDD| convention.
- source: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0957417417304402

### csw_cusum_break (Chu-Stinchcombe-White CUSUM-on-levels break label) [NEW]
- idea: AFML Ch.17.3.2: a standardized-departure structural-break statistic on log-PRICE LEVELS (Homm-Breitung). For each bar compute the max standardized departure of the forward log-price from a reference level, scaled by realized vol; label a structural break (+1 up-break / -1 down-break) when it exceeds the time-dependent critical value (b_0.05=4.6 from the paper). ### hurst_persist [NEW]
- idea: Forward fractal-persistence label. Estimate the Hurst exponent of the FORWARD price window via DFA (or R/S); H>0.5 = persistent/trending, H<0.5 = anti-persistent/mean-reverting. Orthogonal to every inventory labeler: ker reads |net|/gross MAGNITUDE efficiency, perment reads ORDINAL Bandt-Pompe entropy, trend_scan reads OLS t-stat — Hurst reads the SCALING EXPONENT of fluctuation across multiple sub-window sizes, a different multiscale statistic that keeps a volatile-but-self-similar climb perment/ker would cut. ### jump_model (statistical jump-model penalized k-means regime) [NEW]
- idea: Persistent-regime unsupervised labeler: penalized k-means with an explicit JUMP PENALTY (lambda) on state transitions, so regimes are temporally PERSISTENT/low-turnover without HMM's Gaussian-emission assumption. ### sadf_explosive (Supremum-ADF explosiveness label) [NEW]
- idea: AFML Ch.17.4.2: label each bar by whether the forward window is in a price BUBBLE/CRASH (explosive, super-martingale) vs a random walk. SADF spikes when prices grow/collapse faster than a unit root permits; that regime is where 'most participants are caught off guard' and risk/reward is best (de Prado's stated motivation). As a forward-looking unsupervised target: run the supremum-ADF over the forward window of each bar and label +1 if the window is right-tail explosive UP, -1 if explosive DOWN, 0 if random-walk. This is genuinely new vs our cusum_regime (which is a CUSUM mean-shift change-point, NOT an explosiveness/unit-root test) and vs fracdiff (which uses LEFT-tail ADF on features for stationarity, opposite tail, feature side not label side). Targets the bubble-burst transitions our drift/timing labels miss.
- mechanism: On forward window of each bar, fit ADF spec on log-price: Δy_t = a + β·y_{t-1} + Σγ_i·Δy_{t-i} + ε, with backwards-EXPANDING start points t0∈[1,t-τ], fixed right end. SADF = sup over t0 of β/σ_β (the t-stat of β). Right-tail H0: β≤0 (random walk) vs H1: β>0 (explosive). Label = sign of forward log-return when |SADF|>train-fit critical quantile, else 0. Use log prices (Ch.17.4.2.1). For speed/robustness use Quantile-ADF (Q[s_t,0.95]) instead of the raw supremum. LEAK-SAFE: the label may look ahead (it IS the forward target); the only fitted quantity is the SADF critical quantile, fit on TRAIN bars only. ~50 lines numpy: lagged-difference design matrix + closed-form OLS β and σ_β in the expanding inner loop.
- source: AFML Ch.17.4.2 Supremum Augmented Dickey-Fuller (Phillips-Wu-Yu 2011); Ch.17.4.2.4 Quantile-ADF; Ch.17.4.2.1 log vs raw prices. /home/ubuntu/lb/pdfs/AFML.pdf lines 7729-7900 of /tmp/afml_full.txt

### sortino_scan [NEW]
- idea: Downside-risk-adjusted forward trend. Like sharpe_scan (net move / total forward vol) but the denominator is DOWNSIDE deviation only — the std of NEGATIVE forward bar-returns inside the window, with positive bars set to 0 before squaring. A move that climbs through low downside-vol (few/shallow pullbacks) gets a high Sortino even if total vol is high; sharpe_scan would penalize it for the upside vol. This targets exactly the path a long actually feels: smooth-up vs jagged-up. Orthogonal to sharpe_scan (total vol), ker (|net|/gross efficiency) and mfe_mae (extremum asymmetry) — none isolates the DOWNSIDE leg of the forward vol.
- mechanism: Per bar t, horizon H: net = lc[t+H]-lc[t]; per-bar fwd returns r_j = lr[t+1..t+H]; downside_dev = sqrt(mean(min(r_j,0)^2)); SOR = net / (downside_dev*sqrt(H)). Label 1 if |SOR|>=cut and net>0, 0 if net<=0, -1 if |SOR|<cut. cut = TRAIN quantile of |SOR| (q in 0.4..0.7), most-balanced config kept. ~45 lines, cumsum of min(lr,0)^2 makes it O(N) per H. ### wavelet_asym [NEW]
- idea: Wavelet time-asymmetry / shock-class label (direct port of the 2025 PNAS jump taxonomy).
