## Step 0 — First principles: name the money before designing anything

Every strategy is classified into one of five families and the *specific market money* it earns is stated up front: **trend** = inertia money (positive autocorrelation of price changes); **mean-reversion** = overreaction-repair money; **cross-sectional** = relative/ranking money after stripping beta; **arbitrage** = friction money on a spread; **volatility** = sentiment vol-mispricing (mostly a hedge for the other books). His reasoning: each kind of money dictates a different axis, label, and evaluation, and TS vs CS earn *different* money so they can run side by side. The admission test replaces the story: e.g., to trend anything (including a synthetic spread), test the series for positive autocorrelation (DW/ACF) — "even gold and pork" qualify if the spread autocorrelates. Unfamiliar problems are *transformed into the pipeline's domain* rather than solved with new machinery (arbitrage → trend on the spread; regression → binned classification; non-shortable → short-signal-maps-to-flat).

## Step 1 — Asset admission by statistical properties

Rule of thumb: if a small amount of money can move the price, it has no statistical properties (single stocks are explicitly banned — "models won't converge"); if billions can't move it (rebar, gold, broad ETFs, BTC/ETH), it likely does. ETFs are the recommended equity vehicle ("数学统计意义远远高于个股"); crypto is data-dense (2y ≈ 10 domestic years); commodities have *long memory* (production-chain pricing) while equities have short memory — per-asset adaptation (e.g., fractional-memory parameter) is calibrated empirically, never assumed. Drift assets are detected by the label mean: ȳ≈0.7 means the asset is long-only — convert the direction regime, never force balance back to 0.5 by reweighting (he confirms balance-forcing is a trap: AUC rises, Calmar collapses).

## Step 2 — Data acquisition and distributional diagnosis

Minute-level (or coarser) bars suffice — tick data is only for genuinely high-frequency work. Pay for clean data (futures: volume/OI-weighted continuous index series for training/inference, dominant contract for execution, automated rolls). Before any modeling, run the diagnostic that anchors his whole system: compute **1st–5th order distribution differences** of bar closes, standardize, and overlay a standard normal. Time bars show a leptokurtic peak (density ~0.5–0.6 vs 0.4); he proved the **peak height is positively correlated with chop frequency** — trend strategies earn the two fat tails and give it back in the peak. This plot tells you, before training anything, where you earn and where you bleed.

## Step 3 — Custom-axis resampling (the foundation and the ceiling)

Replace the time axis with an information-driven axis (dollar/turnover bars, volume bars, his proprietary C1 price-behavior axis, the advanced "X" axis; price-action axes for series with only price, like spreads). The **objective is uniform information density per bar — normality is only the byproduct/symptom** (he corrects students who optimize kurtosis directly). Key engineering details: (a) the threshold must be **de-scaled / rolling-standardized** — "3bn turnover today is not comparable to 3bn five years ago"; a fixed threshold means the axis itself drifts; (b) calibrate the threshold so bar count matches the time frequency you comfortably trade (the threshold is a *trading-frequency dial*, never an optimization target); (c) cost problems are fixed HERE by enlarging the bar — never downstream by re-tuning labels; (d) overnight gaps need no handling (absorbed inside bars); (e) if phase/start-point sensitivity worries you, **train across N phase offsets** of the resampling; (f) axes must be simple and financially meaningful — GP/symbolic-regression-mined axes are overfitting. Acceptance is the Step-2 diagnostic at matched bar count. The distribution shape also **routes the mechanism**: peak suppressed toward normal → trend models; peak pushed UP with tails shrunk → mean-reversion; bimodal → event-driven. His controlled ablation (everything fixed, axis swapped): RB time-axis Calmar 1.39–2.13 → turnover-axis 2.82–3.31. Reasoning: nobody on earth can separate trend from chop in signal space, so absorb the chop inside the bars — solve the problem one dimension up. Bonus: a private axis is anti-crowding alpha, and factors that "drift" on the time axis often revive on a valid axis.

## Step 4 — Labeling (the linchpin, 承上启下)

Supervised binary trend-segment labels from a **probabilistic clustering model** (GMM-family on log returns; change-point detection equally valid; HMM only *indirectly* — its computation has limitations and it repaints). The entire upswing is 1 and the downswing 0, **pullbacks keep the trend label** — the label encodes the discretionary intent "ride the whole trend." Naive next-bar up/down and raw-return/fixed-horizon labels are "completely wrong" (no statistical property; you label the noise, the model learns the noise). Never 3-class up/down/sideways — that's financial logic, not math logic; the model learns vol buckets instead. The label's **trend degree is a parameter** (his demo: distribution-differencing order 5th→9th = medium→large trends), deliberately producing a *family* of labels of different scales — there is no good/bad label; diversity exists to be combined later. Labels may legitimately use future information (they are training targets); the causal requirement applies only to the deployed model's output. Label validation: visualize labels on the bars, and run label-only ablations (everything else frozen) — "no subjective pre-judgment." He explicitly rejects de Prado's triple-barrier (book 1) and the OLS t-value trend scan (book 2, replicated by his team, "not that good") in favor of his clustering/change-point segments.

## Step 5 — Feature engineering (simple, mathematical, co-designed with the label)

OHLCV is a sufficient base "even for thousands of features." Stack purely mathematical expressions: integer-order differencing (1st = trend, 2nd = acceleration) across multiple windows; **fractional differencing at order 0.7–0.8** (memory + stationarity — his production staple); **sample entropy** (not Shannon: no distribution/encoding assumption) with a **range-based dynamic tolerance r** and — critically — a **fixed wall-clock lookback anchored at the dynamic bar close** so time-dependent features (entropy, vol) don't suffer *feature-meaning drift* when bars span 30 vs 100 minutes; frequency-domain features as a complement, never a replacement (preferred recipe VMD + his NRBO vol-adaptive boundary padding; alternatively CWT+SWT; never plain FFT — the boundary/edge effect is the deployability trap), with windows scaled 50–100× the trade period; regime expressions (HMM states + their entropy, CRF, fractal dimension) **fed as features, never wired as decision rules**; existing factors plug in as features (backward compatibility). Never feed absolute levels. Feel free to inflate dimensionality — reduction follows. In his entropy A/B, swapping 30 of 300 integer-diff features for 30 sample-entropy features lifted RB Calmar ~3.0→4.51 with everything else frozen.

## Step 6 — Feature/factor screening against the LABEL, not returns

"The factor is unimportant; the validation method is what's worth money." Returns are noise; the label is the denoised regime, so score features against it: **information gain** ranking (his other core operator besides differencing), SHAP/LIME filtering chained with PCA, and **add/remove ablation on backtests** (causality, not IC/IR correlation). Individually dead factors may carry live nonlinear combinations, so screen inside the nonlinear pipeline rather than discarding by standalone IC/IR.

## Step 7 — Dimensionality reduction / reconstruction (pre-training overfit suppression)

Reduce before the model: linear PCA (fit on the TRAIN slice only, persist the transform — fitting on all data "is wrong"; find the dimension carrying ~99.99% variance and use it as the **budget** for any reducer), or nonlinear autoencoders (VAE his favorite; recurrent/adversarial/denoising variants; **conditional/supervised CVAE** = label-guided reduction that needs no label at inference — implicit factor selection + dynamic weighting). Sweep the latent dim from 1 up; choose jointly from reconstruction-MSE relative change AND backtest delta; **prefer the lower dimension over the best backtest** (fewer constraint terms = less overfit). After a supervised AE, the final model must be a tree (interpretability). His one confessed real-money loss class: linear-DR *live-inference* pitfalls.

## Step 8 — Model training

Gradient-boosted trees (XGB/LGBM/CatBoost/NGBoost, "Kaggle-style, roughly equivalent") as the final model — interpretable, best on structured data. Linear models can't express the nonlinearity (which is the entire reason for the ML path); LSTM/Transformer only as *feature extractors* feeding the tree, never as the final model; generative/LLM models are banned from decisions (consistency + repeatability are non-negotiable); RL only in quasi-closed environments (HFT/execution), with the action→market feedback severed elsewhere. CV is **matched to the bar type**: resampled near-IID bars legitimize stratified k-fold; time bars demand strictly time-ordered splits. Classification over regression always (bin if you must regress); volatility is the one sane regression target (FIGARCH with per-asset memory; max-entropy spectral for IV).

## Step 9 — Evaluation

Report only **"out-of-sample of out-of-sample"** — a later period untouched by training AND by every selection step (even looking at the inner test split "is a bit like cheating"; combination weights come from TRAIN only). Headline metric: **Calmar** ("we rarely look at Sharpe") because it converts directly into drawdown-budgeted leverage and expected return; plus his proprietary **DA (drawdown area)** = integrated underwater region (recovery speed / "psychological shadow area") and MDT (max DD duration in bars); Sharpe/Sortino ordinal-only. Selection is **anti-cherry-pick**: inspect the whole trained family across the dimensionality grid; if equity curves are roughly consistent, take the LOWER-dimensional member; if the family is wildly uneven, **refuse to select from it at all**. Every claim is demonstrated by controlled single-module ablation (freeze everything, swap one module). Vol forecasts get benchmarked against unconditional volatility; he openly reports nulls ("I found no pattern").

## Step 10 — Secondary (meta) model for sizing

The primary classifier's probability is *confidence, not accuracy* — never size with it. Train a second-level model on top; its probability "indirectly expresses accuracy" and drives add/reduce decisions. Both models infer simultaneously on every signal.

## Step 11 — Model combination (his replacement for regime classification)

Real-time regime identification is impossible (the boundary problem), so he refuses to classify regimes and instead **combines models keyed to different trend scales**: one custom axis, several trend-degree labels, one model each; when they agree (trending) hold, when they disagree (chop) be flat — "a binary classifier always has a signal" is solved structurally. RB demo: singles Calmar ~2.8–3.3 → combination 5.63, MaxDD halved, DA way down, with genuine flat periods (987 trades / 226 flat). Generalized: **multiply axes × label-degrees × feature sets × reducers into many diverse models per instrument** and run multiple axes in parallel — their disagreement nets into *continuous* flat periods and mid-trend capture; within one axis, compute the NET position first so offsetting signals cancel without paying fees. 2–3 models per combo suffice (they tested up to 100 to find the boundary). Combination is the generalization mechanism, and capacity/AUM scaling comes from this diversity, not from perfecting one model.

## Step 12 — Live inference, decay management, risk views

Training completes "only half the work." Live = real-time resampling + real-time inference on each completed custom bar, identical code paths offline/online; the boundary effect (frequency methods AND Markov methods) must be engineered around (NRBO), and "if it can infer the regime online, it can BE the trading model." Decay defense in four layers: alarm on breach of historical MaxDD; run **shadow model groups** live beside the traded one; **incremental retraining every 3–6 months** — but check **label-balance (~0.5) as the data-sufficiency criterion** first, because incremental training can break a working model; or adopt a universal cross-instrument model that structurally never decays (sacrificing per-asset peak returns). Risk is judged from multiple simultaneous independent views — frequency structure (earliest alarm), entropy, HMM probability — different mathematical bases on the same regime are complementary, not redundant.

## Step 13 — Book construction

Diversify the live book across **many symbols × multiple axes** (choppy names sit flat at no cost while trending names pay); map shorts to flat on long-only/drift assets; size by Calmar against the product's drawdown covenant (Calmar 3 × 15% DD budget ⇒ ~45% expected annual). Volatility strategies/forecasts are auxiliary plugins (adaptive rolling thresholds, never fixed numbers) and hedges, not primary alpha.

## Research philosophy (deduped)

- Solve hard problems one dimension up, never head-on: trend vs chop cannot be classified in real time by anyone, so change the representation (the axis) to reduce chop frequency instead of building a smarter classifier; the leptokurtic peak of differenced returns IS chop frequency, and 'how much you lower the peak is how much loss you remove.'
- The data axis is the ceiling of everything downstream: time bars are 'a congenitally flawed child' (non-uniform information density, no statistical properties); the true bar-design objective is uniform per-bar information density — normality/kurtosis are byproducts you must not optimize directly; a private axis is also anti-crowding alpha.
- Labels are the linchpin (承上启下): the model learns exactly the label's characteristics, so label noise = learning noise; labels must be statistically meaningful trend segments (pullbacks keep the trend label), deliberately discrete and binary (3-class up/down/sideways is financial logic, not math logic); labels are training targets and may legitimately use future information — causality applies to the deployed signal, not the label.
- Overfitting is suppressed at EVERY pipeline stage, not by one late regularizer: axis simplicity, denoised labels, dimensionality reduction (fewer constraint terms), parsimony in model selection (prefer the lower-dimensional member of a consistent family; refuse to select from inconsistent families), and combination diversity.
- The validation system is the asset, not the signal: 'the factor is unimportant; the testing method is what's worth money' — each firm's core secret is HOW it tests; validate features/factors against labels, not noisy raw returns, and prefer causal add/remove ablation over correlation (IC/IR).
- Holistic coordination (统筹/协同) is the supreme skill: every stage is coupled (axis → admissible CV; label → feature co-design; reduction → live inference), so plan globally and implement stepwise; knowing one stage in isolation 'doesn't mean much' — institutions silo researchers precisely so only the PM owns the whole chain.
- Online computability is the bar for deployability: global-optimum algorithms (HMM, change-point, frequency decompositions) repaint at the live edge — route them to labeling/features, never to trading; boundary/edge effects must be engineered around, and anything that could compute its regime accurately online would itself be the strategy.
- Consistency and repeatability are non-negotiable: same data in, identical output and confidence out — discriminative models only, no generative/LLM models in the decision loop, RL only in quasi-closed environments.
- There is no holy-grail single model or label: diversity (label trend-degrees, axes, reducers) exists to be COMBINED; combination is the generalization mechanism, the indirect solution to regime identification (agreement = trend, disagreement = stay flat), and the route to AUM capacity.
- Traditional trees are enough; the frontier is in axes, labels, features and validation, not model architecture; neural networks belong inside feature extraction/reduction only; pure mathematical logic everywhere — the model doesn't know what industry it is, so abandon the finance narrative.
- Evaluate honestly and decision-relevantly: report only out-of-sample-of-out-of-sample (the inner test split is 'a bit like cheating'); judge by Calmar (it converts directly into drawdown-budgeted leverage) plus drawdown AREA and duration (the investor's 'psychological shadow'); demonstrate every claim with controlled single-module ablations; benchmark against the naive baseline; report nulls openly.
- Asset physics over tuning: assets either have statistical properties or they don't (single stocks don't; ETFs/liquid futures/crypto do); mechanism fit (trend vs reversion vs buy-hold/long-only), memory length, and direction regime are intrinsic to the asset and detected empirically (label mean ≈0.7 ⇒ long-only), never forced by rebalancing tricks.
- Work flows forward only and complexity is front-loaded: trading frequency and cost-viability are fixed at the resampling stage (enlarge the bar, never re-tune labels after costs); if axis+label are right, features can stay simple.
- Plan for decay before it happens: monitor DD breaches, run shadow models, retrain on a 3–6 month cadence gated by label-balance data-sufficiency (incremental retraining can BREAK a working model), or choose a universal model that structurally avoids decay at the cost of peak returns.
- Methods are bought with losses, and the canon is replicated before trusted: NRBO came from residual plots of failed padding methods; his team replicated and rejected de Prado's triple-barrier and t-value trend scan; 'many critical details you only realize after losing a lot of money' — and real edges are not taught by your direct competitors.
- Standardize everything compared across time (bar thresholds, features — de-scale/去量纲) and never use fixed magic thresholds: operational thresholds must be adaptive rolling quantities, 'alive, not dead.'
